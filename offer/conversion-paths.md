# Conversion Paths — Sentinel Ops

> After every pilot, three paths forward. This doc makes each one clear — who it fits, what it includes, and how to position it so the prospect chooses the right option for their situation.

---

## The 3 Paths at a Glance

| Path | What It Is | Who It's For | Price |
|------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| **A: Continue Managed Service** | Ongoing monitoring + hardening + triage | Happy with pilot, want continuous coverage | RM 1,500–3,500/month |
| **B: Expand Scope** | More servers or extended pilot before committing | Need more proof, growing environment | RM 3,000–8,000/pilot extension |
| **C: Stop Here** | Pilot was the destination | Satisfied with baseline, no ongoing need | No ongoing commitment |

---

## Path A: Continue — Managed Service ✅ (Recommended)

**What it is:** Convert the pilot into an ongoing monthly retainer. Sentinel Ops continues monitoring, hardening, alert triage, and reporting — as a running service.

**Who it fits:**
- The prospect confirmed value during the pilot
- They have ongoing compliance needs (PDPA, BNM, MCMC)
- They have a growing server environment
- They want a security team on call, not just a one-time project
- Decision-maker was present and engaged throughout the pilot

**Who it's NOT for:**
- Budget is genuinely not there for recurring cost
- They're winding down or selling the business
- They want to build internal security team instead
- They only needed a point-in-time assessment

**Pricing tiers:**

| Tier | Monthly | Scope | Best For |
|------|---------|-------|----------|
| Watcher | RM 1,500 | Up to 5 servers, monthly digest + vulnerability scan | Small (5–15 employees), basic compliance |
| Guardian | RM 2,500 | Up to 15 servers, weekly digest + triage + hardening | Growing (15–50 employees), active compliance need |
| Fortress | RM 4,500+ | Unlimited servers, 24/7 alerting + dedicated analyst + SLA | Mid-market, multiple sites, active threats |

**What happens on day 31:**
- Monitoring continues seamlessly (no gap — agents stay live)
- Report schedule: weekly digest + monthly management report
- Hardening maintenance: new CIS checks applied as they come out
- Alert triage: human review of all events, false positive tuning monthly
- WhatsApp/email support: business hours response within 4 hours

**Conversion approach:**

> "You saw what we did over 30 days. The difference between a pilot and an ongoing service is continuity — agents stay live, we keep watching, and you have a team that knows your environment. At RM [tier price]/month, you get [key deliverables]. The alternative is doing this again from scratch in 6 months when something happens."

**Close language:**
> "If you want to continue, the simplest next step is to sign today — we can have everything running from tomorrow with no gap in coverage. Or we can start on [date]. Which works for you?"

## Path B: Expand Scope

**What it is:** Extend the pilot with more servers, more coverage, or more time before committing to a full managed service. Usually a 30-60 day extended pilot.

**Who it fits:**
- Prospect is happy but wants to prove value across more infrastructure before committing
- They have a multi-site or multi-environment setup (e.g., head office + branch offices)
- They recently added servers and want those covered too
- They're in the middle of an acquisition or expansion
- The board needs more convincing before budget is approved

**Who it's NOT for:**
- They already saw enough with the initial pilot scope
- Budget is already committed elsewhere and will be reviewed next quarter
- They're already decided but need internal procurement to clear

**Pricing:**

| Scope | Duration | Price | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|-------|
| Add 5 more servers | 30 days | RM 1,500 extension | Same as pilot add-on |
| Full infrastructure scan | 30 days | RM 3,000 extension | All servers, no hardening |
| Extended monitoring only (no hardening) | 60 days | RM 2,000 | Triage only, biweekly reports |

**What happens during the extension:**
- Agents deployed to new servers
- Monitoring live on expanded scope
- Biweekly updates instead of weekly
- Findings report at end of extension period
- Full pilot review call at end

**Conversion approach:**

> "I understand you want more visibility before committing. Here's what I'd suggest: we extend coverage to [new scope] for another 30 days — same price as the initial pilot, same structure. You get the full picture across your environment before deciding. At the end of the 30 days, we run the same review and you decide."

**Close language:**
> "The extended pilot keeps everything running while you get the internal sign-off you need. If at the end you decide to continue, we roll the extension cost into the first 3 months. If not, you walk away with a full findings report for your environment. Fair?"

## Path C: Stop After Pilot — Walk Away Clean ❌

**What it is:** The pilot was completed, the prospect has the baseline scan and hardening, and they choose not to continue with a managed service. No hard feelings.

**Who it fits:**
- They achieved what they needed from the pilot (one-time assessment, board presentation, compliance audit)
- Budget is not available for recurring cost right now
- They're planning to build internal security capability
- The pilot revealed their risk profile is lower than expected (less urgency)
- They need to go through procurement before committing to recurring cost

**What we give them at the end:**
- Final management report (they keep this)
- Operations log (all hardening changes, all findings)
- Post-pilot security brief (1-page summary for board/internal use)
- Permission to re-contact in 3 months ("the door is open")

**What we DON'T do:**
- Leave agents running unless they ask to keep monitoring (they can, but it's on them)
- Provide free support after the pilot ends
- Give them ongoing access to the dashboard (close the account after 30 days)

**Post-pilot offboarding:**
- Send final report + ops log within 48 hours of decision
- Send warm WhatsApp message: "Thanks for the pilot. You now have a clear picture of where you stand. Our door is open whenever you need us."
- Add to 3-month follow-up list
- Add to 6-month "check in" WhatsApp sequence

**What to say when they choose Path C:**

> "That's completely fair. The pilot did its job — you now know exactly where you stand, you have the hardening applied, and you have a clear report. If anything changes — a security incident, an audit, a new requirement — come back and we'll pick up where we left off. I'll keep you in the loop for anything that might be relevant to your environment."

**Do NOT:**
- Pressure them to continue
- Offer deep discounts out of desperation (devalues the service)
- Promise free continued support
- Leave them with the impression that stopping means the relationship is over

## Decision Matrix — Which Path to Push

Use this to guide the conversation toward the right outcome:

| Condition | Recommended Path |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Budget confirmed, compliance pressure active | A: Managed Service |
| Budget confirmed, but board needs more convincing | B: Expand Scope (to show board) |
| Budget uncertain, timeline Q3+ | C: Stop, follow up in 3 months |
| Budget < RM 1,000/month | A: Watcher tier (if fits) or C |
| Pilot found serious findings (active threat) | A: Managed Service (urgency created) |
| Pilot found low-risk environment | A or C (value proposition is harder) |
| Decision-maker not present at review call | B: Extend (schedule another call first) |
| Multiple stakeholders need to approve | B: Expand scope gives them more time |

---

## Follow-Up Cadence

After the pilot review call, regardless of outcome:

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 30 | Final report + conversion offer sent |
| Day 31 | WhatsApp follow-up: "Thanks for today — [1-line summary + next steps]" |
| Day 33 | If no response: "Have you had a chance to look at the options?" |
| Day 37 | If no response: "No pressure — just checking in. Let us know if you need anything." |
| Day 60 | If Path C: warm message "How's things? Anything changed on the security front?" |
| Day 90 | If Path C: "3 months in — wanted to check in. Any security priorities coming up?" |
| Day 180 | If Path C: Annual check-in. "Has anything security-related come up?" |

## Quick-Reference Close Lines

**Pushing Path A:**
> "If Sentinel Ops worked for 30 days, why would you stop on Day 31?"
> "The alternative is doing this again from scratch in 6 months when something happens. Keep us running."
> "You have a team looking after your servers. Now you need a team looking after your security."

**Pushing Path B:**
> "Extend the pilot while you get internal sign-off. No gap in coverage."
> "30 more days gives you the full picture — then you decide with confidence."
> "If you need to show the board, let's give them a bigger dataset to look at."

**Letting go of Path C:**
> "The pilot was worth it either way — you have the hardening, the report, the visibility."
> "No hard feelings. The door is open whenever you need us."
> "I'll put you in for a 3-month check-in. If anything changes before then — incident, audit, new requirement — reach out immediately."
