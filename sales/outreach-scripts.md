# Outreach Scripts — Sentinel Ops

## Philosophy
Outreach should feel like a helpful conversation, not a sales pitch. We lead with what we understand about their situation, not what we want to sell. Scripts are starting points — adapt based on what you learn in the conversation.

---

## Initial Contact (Cold Outreach / Warm Intro)

### Template 1: Referral / Introduction
> "Hi [Name], [Referrer] suggested I reach out — we work with [their company/industry] on security monitoring and server hardening. I wanted to see if this is relevant for your business, and if not, I understand completely.
>
> Briefly: we help SMEs get enterprise-grade security monitoring without the enterprise price tag. We do vulnerability scanning, server hardening, alert triage, and monthly management reports. Most clients come to us when they're either preparing for compliance, had a close call, or want to stop paying for tools they don't understand.
>
> Is now a bad time, or do you have 5 minutes for a quick call?"

### Template 2: LinkedIn / Email Cold Outbound
**Subject:** Quick question about [their company]'s security posture

> Hi [Name],
>
> I wanted to reach out because we work with companies like [industry] on managed security operations — specifically helping them get visibility into their server infrastructure, reduce alert noise, and have a team that actually owns security outcomes.
>
> Most of our clients come to us when:
> - They have an IT team but no dedicated security person
> - They've had a scare or near-miss they don't want to repeat
> - They're preparing for PDPA compliance or a client security review
> - They're using tools like Microsoft 365 or AWS but aren't confident their config is right
>
> Would a 15-minute call make sense to see if there's a fit? No pressure — if the timing isn't right, I completely understand.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

### Template 3: WhatsApp (for warm leads / referrals)
> Hi [Name] 👋 [Referrer] gave me your contact. I work with businesses on security monitoring and server hardening — quick question: is security something your team is actively managing right now, or is it more of a "we should look at this" item?
>
> If it's the latter, we run a 30-day pilot at RM 2,000 where we scan your servers, apply hardening, and give you a management report at the end. No long-term commitment. Would that be useful?
>
> Happy to jump on a quick call if you want to know more.

## Discovery Questions (After Initial Interest)

These questions help qualify the prospect and understand their actual situation before proposing anything.

**Situation:**
- "Can you tell me a bit about your business — what kind of infrastructure do you have?"
- "How many servers or endpoints are you running?"
- "Where are they hosted — on-prem, cloud, or hybrid?"
- "Do you have an internal IT team or do you use a vendor?"

**Problems / Drivers:**
- "What's driving the interest in security right now? Is it a specific event, a compliance requirement, or more proactive?"
- "Have you had any security incidents or near-misses in the past?"
- "What does your team currently use for security? Antivirus? Cloud provider tools? Anything else?"
- "If we found something high-risk, how quickly would you want to know — and who would act on it?"

**Decision:**
- "Who else would be involved in a decision like this?"
- "What's your timeline — is there a deadline or event driving this?"
- "What would a successful outcome look like for you in 3 months?"

## Follow-Up Templates

### Follow-up after no response (3–5 days)
> Hi [Name], just following up on my earlier message. No pressure if this isn't relevant — I just wanted to make sure it didn't get lost. If you're happy with your current security setup, totally understand. If not, happy to chat.

### After initial call / meeting
> Hi [Name], great talking with you. As discussed, here's a summary of what we can do:
>
> - [Brief restate of their problem]
> - [What we'd deliver]
> - [Pricing tier that fits]
>
> Let me know if you'd like to proceed with a pilot or if you have questions. Happy to adjust scope if needed.

---

## Closing Questions

When they're interested but not committing:
- "What would help you feel more confident to move forward?"
- "Is there anything holding you back that I haven't addressed?"
- "Would it help to start small — maybe a pilot on just 2–3 servers — to see how it works before committing to more?"
- "What's your decision-making process look like for something like this?"

---

## Notes on Tone

- **Be helpful, not pushy.** If someone says no, thank them and move on. Don't double-text more than once after a "not interested."
- **Be specific.** "Your server has SSH exposed on port 22 with password auth" is more compelling than "you might have security issues."
- **Be honest.** If we can't help them, say so. A referral is better than a failed engagement.
- **Be patient.** Security buying decisions can take 2–8 weeks. Don't rush, but don't go quiet either.
