# WhatsApp Pitch — Sentinel Ops

## Opening Messages (Pick One)

### Casual Intro
> Hey [Name] 👋 saw your company [doing X] — been thinking about whether you have security monitoring in place for your servers. Are you currently looking after that, or is it something you've been meaning to sort out?

### Referral Style
> Hi [Name], [Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out. I work with businesses on security monitoring and server hardening — basically helping SMEs get the kind of security coverage you'd normally need a full SOC team for. Can I ask — is security something you're actively managing right now?

### Direct
> Hi [Name] — I'm building a managed security service for Malaysian SMEs. Short version: we monitor your servers, apply hardening, filter out alert noise, and give you a management report every month. Starting with a 30-day pilot on a few servers — RM 2,000, no long-term commitment. Interested in knowing more?

---

## After They Respond

**If they say yes / interested:**
> Great. Here's what the pilot looks like:
> - We scan your servers, apply hardening
> - Monitor for 30 days with human alert triage
> - Give you a management report at the end
> - You see real findings, risk reduction, clear next steps
> - No ongoing commitment unless you want to continue
>
> Cost: RM 2,000 for the 30-day pilot.
>
> If that sounds useful, we can start with just 2–3 servers. Want to jump on a quick 15-min call to see if it's a fit?

**If they say "what do you do":**
> We help businesses that have servers (Linux, cloud, whatever) but no dedicated security person watching over them. We set up monitoring (Wazuh-based), apply hardening, filter the noise so you're not drowning in alerts, and give you a monthly report you can actually use. Think of us as an outsourced security team without the enterprise price tag.

**If they say "we have an IT vendor":**
> That's totally normal — most of our clients do. We're not replacing your IT vendor, we're adding a security layer on top. Your IT team handles availability and performance; we handle security posture, hardening, and compliance. They're complementary. Does your IT team have a security person specifically, or are they doing security on top of everything else?

**If they say "it's a bit expensive":**
> I understand. The pilot is designed to be low-commitment — RM 2,000 for 30 days, then you decide. We also have a lower tier (RM 1,500/month) that covers monthly scanning and reports without the full monitoring. But honestly, if budget is tight right now, the pilot is a good way to see what you'd get without committing to ongoing cost. And if we find something critical in the first scan, that's worth knowing regardless.

**If they say "let me think about it":**
> No problem. A couple of questions though — is there a specific deadline or event that's making you look at this now? And who else would be involved in the decision? Just so I know the timeline, and so I can send you the right kind of summary to help you make the case internally.

## Closing Lines

**When they seem ready:**
> "So if we can show you a real finding in your first scan — something your current setup would miss — does that help you decide?"

**When they need approval:**
> "Would it help if I prepared a one-page summary you could share with your boss / the board?"

**When they want to start small:**
> "Let's start with just 2 servers for the pilot — prove it works before committing to more. You can always add more after."

---

## Tone Notes

- **Be human.** Not a sales script. Adapt based on how they talk.
- **Be specific.** "Your server has SSH on port 22 exposed to the internet with password auth" is more compelling than "you might have security issues."
- **Be honest.** If we can't help them, say so.
- **Be patient.** Security buying decisions take 2–8 weeks. Don't rush, don't go quiet.
