# Follow-Up Sequence — Sentinel Ops

## Philosophy

Follow-up is where deals are made or lost. Most first contacts don't convert immediately — especially for security services, which require trust and budget clarity. The follow-up sequence keeps us present without being annoying.

**Rule:** Never follow up more than 3 times after the initial message. If no response after 3, move on.

---

## Sequence Overview

| Day | Action | Channel | Purpose |
|-----|--------|---------|---------|
| Day 0 | Initial outreach | WhatsApp / Email | Introduce, create interest |
| Day 2 | Follow-up #1 | Same channel | Gentle nudge |
| Day 5 | Value add | Email | Share relevant insight |
| Day 10 | Follow-up #2 | WhatsApp / Email | Decision check |
| Day 14 | Final check | WhatsApp / Email | Last attempt, clear close |

---

## Day 0 — Initial Outreach

Use one of the templates from `whatsapp-pitch.md` or `outreach-scripts.md`. Personalize based on what you know about the prospect.

---

## Day 2 — Follow-Up #1

**If no response from initial message:**

> Hi [Name], just following up on my message from [X] days ago. No pressure if the timing isn't right — I just wanted to make sure it didn't get lost.
>
> If security monitoring is something you're actively looking at right now, happy to share what a 30-day pilot would look like for your setup. If you're already covered, totally understand — just let me know and I'll stop following up.

**If they responded but didn't convert:**
Acknowledge their situation and add relevant context:

> Hey [Name], thanks for getting back. Just to recap what we discussed: you're looking at security for [N] servers, mainly concerned about [their concern]. The pilot would start by scanning those servers and showing you what we find — typically in the first week we find things clients didn't know about.
>
> The RM 2,000 pilot gives you 30 days of monitoring, hardening, and a management report. Would it help if I sent over the one-pager so you can share it with [decision-maker]?

---

## Day 5 — Value Add

Don't ask for anything. Share something useful:

**Option A — Relevant article:**
> Hey [Name], came across this article about [topic — e.g., "a Malaysian logistics company got hit by ransomware because SSH was exposed"] — thought it might be relevant given what you told me about [their situation]. [link if useful]
>
> The pattern we see repeatedly is: exposed ports, weak SSH configs, no one watching the logs. Those are all fixable — but you have to know about them first.

**Option B — PDPA deadline reminder:**
> [Name], quick note — PDPA enforcement has been increasing in Malaysia. Companies that don't have documented security monitoring are increasingly exposed to regulatory risk. If you're in a sector that handles personal data (finance, healthcare, e-commerce), it's worth getting ahead of this before an audit shows up.
>
> Our pilot is designed to give you that documentation trail and visibility — without a big upfront commitment.

**Option C — Simple check-in:**
> [Name], checking in briefly. Have you had a chance to look at the pilot proposal? Happy to answer any questions or adjust scope to fit your situation better.

---

## Day 10 — Follow-Up #2

**If no response to Day 5:**

> Hi [Name], I'll be honest — I know security isn't always the most urgent thing on your plate. But the reason I keep following up is that we've seen companies get caught out by exactly the things a pilot would find: exposed SSH, outdated software with known CVEs, no monitoring on database servers.
>
> The pilot costs RM 2,000 and runs 30 days. If we find nothing, you have a clean baseline. If we find something — you know what to fix before it becomes a problem.
>
> Is it worth a 15-minute call to see if it's a fit?

---

## Day 14 — Final Check

**Last attempt before moving to cold list:**

> Hi [Name], this will be my last follow-up on this — I don't want to keep bothering you if it's not relevant.
>
> If you're happy with your current security setup, great — I genuinely mean that. But if there's any part of you that's thinking "we should probably look at this more seriously," the pilot is the lowest-risk way to do it. RM 2,000, 30 days, we handle everything.
>
> If you want to explore, reply and I'll send the simple SoW. If not, I'll leave you alone and wish you well.

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up

After a discovery call or demo:

**Same day:**
> [Name], great talking with you today. Here's the summary:
> - What we discussed: [their situation]
> - What we agreed: [pilot scope / next steps]
> - What happens next: [timeline]
>
> I'll send the SoW tomorrow. Questions? Just reply here.

**Day 3 after meeting (no proposal signed):**
> Hey [Name], just checking — did you get a chance to look at the proposal I sent? Any questions I can answer? Happy to jump on a quick call if it would help.

**Day 7 after meeting:**
> [Name], following up on the pilot proposal. I know these things can take time to get approval — let me know where things stand. If the timing isn't right, I can check back in [X weeks]. If you need something adjusted in the proposal, just say the word.

---

## After Proposal Sent — No Response

**Day 3:**
> [Name], just wanted to make sure you received the proposal I sent on [date]. No pressure — if you've got questions or want to discuss, I'm here. If the numbers don't work, we can look at adjusting scope.

**Day 10:**
> [Name], I haven't heard back and I want to check — is the proposal something you're still actively considering? I ask because if the timing isn't right, I can check back in a month or two. But if there's something I can do to help you move forward, I'd rather know.

---

## Escalation / Urgency Triggers

If you sense urgency (they mentioned an audit, breach, client requiring security cert, etc.), move faster:

**Day 0:** Send full proposal immediately  
**Day 2:** Call (not just WhatsApp)  
**Day 5:** Offer to come on-site for a quick meeting

---

## Re-Engagement (Old Leads)

If you have a lead that went cold (4+ weeks, no response):

> Hey [Name], it's been a while — wanted to check in. A lot has happened in the security space in [time period]. We've been running Sentinel Ops pilots for [X] months and the results have been solid — companies are finding real vulnerabilities in the first week.
>
> Is now a better time to revisit the security conversation, or should I check back later? Either way, no pressure.
