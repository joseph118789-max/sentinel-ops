# Post-Demo Follow-Up — Sentinel Ops

> Send after every demo, whether it's a live demo, a recorded walkthrough, or a shared report. Timing and tone depend on what happened in the demo.

---

## If Demo Went Well (They Engaged, Asked Questions)

**Send within 2 hours:**

> Hi [name], thanks for making time today. It was good to show you what continuous monitoring actually looks like in practice — not just a dashboard screenshot, but real alerts, real findings, real hardening.
>
> The demo showed: [1 specific thing from the demo that resonated — e.g., "how we caught the brute-force attempt within seconds" or "the CIS scan picked up 18 config issues on the demo server"].
>
> Here's what I'd suggest next: a 30-day pilot on [Company Name's] environment. We deploy on your actual servers, do the real baseline scan, apply hardening, and monitor for a month. You see exactly what we found for your environment specifically — not a generic demo.
>
> It's RM 2,000 for the month, no ongoing commitment. If it works, you decide if you want to continue.
>
> Does that make sense? I'm free [day/time] for a quick call to scope it out.

## If They Said "Looks Good, We'll Think About It"

**Send same day:**

> Hi [name], good speaking today. As discussed, here are the key things to think about:
>
> 1. **The finding gap** — you currently have no visibility into what's happening on your servers. We showed exactly what that looks like.
> 2. **The hardening window** — every day without CIS hardening is a window that's open. The pilot locks it down within week 2.
> 3. **The review call** — at day 30, we walk through exactly what we found, what we changed, and what staying on looks like. No pressure.
>
> If you decide to move forward, my availability is [dates]. Happy to schedule a quick scope call this week.
>
> If it's not the right time — just let me know. I can follow up in 3 months. No push.

## If They Raised Specific Objections During Demo

**Send within 2 hours — address each objection directly:**

> Hi [name], following up on the demo. You raised [specific objection — e.g., "we use a cloud provider so we're covered"] — I wanted to address that directly:
>
> [Your response to the objection — 2-3 sentences, factual, not defensive]
>
> [Then add the standard pilot proposal CTA]

**Common objections and responses:**

*"We use AWS/Azure, they handle security"*
> Cloud providers secure the infrastructure layer — the OS, the apps, the configs are your responsibility. The Capital Optical breach was a config issue inside AWS. We cover the layer the cloud provider doesn't.

*"We already have an IT vendor"*
> We're a security overlay, not an IT replacement. The vendor manages infrastructure, we manage security outcomes. We coordinate with them, not around them.

*"We're too small to be a target"*
> Most attacks are automated — botnets scanning the entire internet. They don't know or care how small you are. Smaller companies get hit precisely because they assume they're invisible. We're not expensive because you're a big target; we're affordable because you're not.

*"We had an audit last year and passed"*
> Audits are point-in-time. They catch what was wrong on the day of the audit. We monitor continuously. Happy to show you what's happened since your last audit — it might surprise you.

## If Demo Was Soft / They Didn't Engage Much

**Send same day — short, low-pressure:**

> Hi [name], thanks for the time today. I know security can feel like a lot to take in — the good news is, you don't have to do anything with it right now.
>
> If anything changes on your end — a security incident, an audit requirement, a client asking you to prove your security posture — come back and we'll take another look.
>
> I'll keep you in the loop for anything that might be relevant to Malaysian SMEs specifically.
