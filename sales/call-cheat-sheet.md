---

## ━━━ SLIDE-BY-SLIDE TALK TRACK ━━━

### Slide 1 — Title
"Sentinel Ops is a lean managed cybersecurity service focused on practical outcomes — monitoring, hardening, and reporting — for teams that need security discipline without heavyweight overhead."

### Slide 2 — The Problem
"Most growing companies don't have a tooling problem — they have an operational problem. Alerts create noise, hardening is inconsistent, compliance pressure keeps increasing, and hiring a real SOC team is unrealistic. That's the gap we're solving."

### Slide 3 — Our Answer
"We provide a lightweight managed layer: monitoring for critical systems, triage to separate signal from noise, baseline hardening to improve actual posture, and management-ready reporting so the customer has something usable — not just dashboards."

### Slide 4 — Who It's For
"We're intentionally focused on SMEs and growing teams with Linux or cloud workloads, especially where customer security pressure or PDPA-style compliance pressure exists. We're not trying to be everything to everyone."

### Slide 5 — How It Works
"The service flow is simple. First, we onboard a limited scope. Then we detect and prioritize meaningful issues. Then we harden where appropriate. Then we report clearly and guide next actions. Small scope, fast visibility, practical follow-through."

### Slide 6 — Demo Proof
"We've already built and tested the MVP flow internally — agent onboarding, event collection, hardening checks, and reporting. So the first customer pilot is not theoretical. We already have a working operational model."
⚠️ **If asked:** "These are internal demo numbers — not customer production data."

### Slide 7 — 30-Day Pilot
"We start with a tightly scoped 30-day pilot. Usually 3 to 10 critical Linux systems. The goal is to deploy quickly, surface real issues, show hardening improvements, and end with a clear management-ready summary and continuation decision."

### Slide 8 — Pricing
"We keep pricing simple. Start narrow, prove value, then continue at the service level that matches the environment. The point is not to oversell — it's to find the smallest commercial scope that is still useful."

### Slide 9 — CTA
"The next step is not a giant commitment. It's a short discovery call to understand whether the environment is a fit for a limited pilot. If it is, we scope the pilot and move fast."

## ━━━ OBJECTION HANDLING ━━━

### "Why not just buy a tool?"
"Tools create visibility, but they don't create operating discipline on their own. The problem for most teams isn't access to dashboards — it's having monitoring, prioritization, hardening, and reporting run consistently enough to matter."

### "Why not hire internally?"
"That's a valid path, but for many SMEs the cost of building even a basic internal security operations function is much higher than starting with a narrow managed model. Sentinel Ops is designed to cover that gap without forcing a heavy headcount decision."

### "Why start with a pilot?"
"Because it reduces risk for both sides. We keep the scope narrow, prove value quickly, and use real findings and improvements to decide whether ongoing service makes sense."

### "Can you cover [out-of-scope thing]?"
"We're focused on Linux and cloud environments running practical workloads. For broader consulting or regulated financial environments requiring formal SOC coverage, we're not the right fit initially — and I'd rather tell you that upfront."
